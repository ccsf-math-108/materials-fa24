This support page will help connect you with the available support for MATH 108: Foundations of Data Science, a newer course at City College of San Francisco.

The Math Lab offers free MATH 108 tutoring in person and virtually through a team of professional teaching assistants and trained student tutors.

Math Lab Details

  • The Math Lab is located in JDVL 808 on the north side of the Ocean Avenue Campus just off Judson Ave.
  • The Math Lab's Fall 2024 in-person tutoring hours are:
    • Monday through Thursday from 9:00 am until 5:00 pm
    • Friday from 9:00 am until 2:00 pm.

Math Lab Staff Supporting MATH 108

  • Calvin Chen, Charles Hutchins, Ed Lee, Jesús Flores Sánchez, and Sophia Sun support MATH 108.
  • There are some virtual appointments with the Math Lab tutors:
    • Use the Canvas Calendar through the Online Math Lab course shell to find and make appointments.
    • Consult the Canvas Student Help Guide on how to find and make appointments.
    • Tutors will update their online availability weekly.

Online Math Lab

The Math Lab offers student services online, too! Self-enroll in the Online Math Lab canvas shell to access course-specific resources such as guided learning activities and to make a virtual appointment with our tutors. These resources include support for courses in Pre-Algebra, Beginning Algebra, Intermediate Algebra, and Pre-statistics.

Math Lab Coordinator

For more information about the Math Lab, contact Charles Hutchins, the Math Lab Coordinator, at 415-452-5575 or chutchin@ccsf.edu.

Data Ambassadors offer weekly evening homework support sessions through Zoom. Additionally, they provide mentorship about studying Data Science beyond MATH 108 and the transfer process. These ambassadors consist of former community college students currently studying at UC Berkeley along with current CCSF students and staff. The weekly meeting details will be posted in the Canvas Calendar.

For questions or comments about this program, please contact Kseniya Usovich (k_usovich@berkeley.edu).

For the past few years, students like you have collaborated to create and maintain a student-focused Discord channel. The original intention of the channel was to create a space just for students to work on the course material and connect over all things related to Data Science. You will find current students needing help and some other students offering help. Past students tend to drop by to volunteer their time to help you now and then. You might see some of the tutors stop by when their schedules allow. Our Math Lab tutor Ed Lee is the current channel administrator

MATH 108 heavily relies on Jupyter notebooks and Python programming. Below are some key resources:

  • Jupyter Project - Official Jupyter documentation and resources.
  • Python Documentation - Official Python documentation for all versions.
  • Pandas Documentation - Guide for the Pandas library, used extensively in data manipulation.
  • Matplotlib Documentation - Documentation for Matplotlib, a plotting library in Python.
  • Seaborn Documentation - Advanced data visualization library based on Matplotlib.
  • Altair Documentation - Declarative statistical visualization library for Python.

The Computer Science Tutor Squad is a group of experienced students who provide support in computer science courses. They offer help with course content, programming assignments, and understanding key concepts in Python and other languages. For MATH 108 students, they can assist with programming challenges in Python and Jupyter notebooks.

Details for the tutor squad sessions can be found on the CCSF CS Tutor Squad webpage.
